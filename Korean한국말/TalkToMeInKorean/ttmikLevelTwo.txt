ㄹ/을 거예요
Verb + ㄹ/을 거예요 = Future tense
How to determine whether to use ㄹ거예요 or 을 거예요
1. Verb stems ending with a vowel (보다, 가다, 자다) are followed by ᄅ 거예요.
2. Verb stems ending with a consonant (먹다, 찾다, 붙다) are followed by 을 거예요.
(**There is no complex reason for this. It’s just for the ease of pronunciation.)
3. Exception: Verb stems already ending with ᄅ at the end (놀다, 멀다, 살다) are followed just by 거예요.

갈거예요: I'm going to go. I will go
네일 갈거예요: I'm going to go tomorrow
뭐 할거예요? : What are you going to do?
어제 할 거예요? When are you going to do it?
이거 정말 할 거예요? : Are you really going to do it?
티셔츠 입을 거예요 : I'm going to wear a t-shirt

There are many ways to do counters in Korean 
연필 한 자루 = 1 pencil
연필 한 개 = 1 pencil 
In this lesson, remember these two most frequently used counters, 개 and 명.
Numbers + 개 (counter for things) 
1 = 하나 --> 한 개
2 = 둘 --> 두 개
3 = 셋 --> 세 개
4 = 넷 --> 네 개
** Remember this irregularity rule for the numbers 1, 2, 3, 4, and 20? 5 = 다섯 --> 다섯 개 
6 = 여섯 --> 여섯 개 7 = 일곱 --> 일곱 개 8 = 여덟 --> 여덟 개 9 = 아홉 --> 아홉 개 10 = 열 --> 열 개 
One apple = 사과 한 개 	
Two stones = 돌 두개
Five balls = 공 다섯 게
