ㄹ/을 거예요
Verb + ㄹ/을 거예요 = Future tense
How to determine whether to use ㄹ거예요 or 을 거예요
1. Verb stems ending with a vowel (보다, 가다, 자다) are followed by ᄅ 거예요.
2. Verb stems ending with a consonant (먹다, 찾다, 붙다) are followed by 을 거예요.
(**There is no complex reason for this. It’s just for the ease of pronunciation.)
3. Exception: Verb stems already ending with ᄅ at the end (놀다, 멀다, 살다) are followed just by 거예요.

갈거예요: I'm going to go. I will go
네일 갈거예요: I'm going to go tomorrow

어제 할 거예요? When are you going to do it?

There are a few different ways to say “only” in Korean but the most basic way of saying it is adding 만 after a noun, pronoun, or the noun form (-기)  of a verb

Adding 만 after noun forms of verbs 
In order to add 만 after a verb, you need to change the verb into the noun form using 기 and add 만하다. Translated this means “ I only do + ~ing” 
듣기 = listening 
듣기만 하다 = to only listen 
듣기만 했어요: I only listened 
오늘만 일찍 왔어요 : I got here early only today 
맥주만 주만 했어요 : I only ordered beer 
왜 이것만 샀어요 ? : Why did you buy only this?
어제 놀기만 했어요: I did nothing but played 
영화는 집에서만 봐요 : I watch movies only at home
